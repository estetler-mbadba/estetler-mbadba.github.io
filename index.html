01 The Operating System

People.  Process.  Stewardship.

PeopleHiring, onboarding, employee relations, unions, supervisors, morale.

ProcessTransportation, maintenance, food service, documentation, rhythms.

StewardshipBudgets, facilities, public trust, taxpayer dollars, long-term sustainability.

I build people-first systems that make complex work clearer, fairer, and more sustainable.

02 How I Make Decisions

Diagnose before you design.

My first move is not to redesign. It's to understand what is already working, where the friction is, and what the people closest to the work already know.

03 How I Partner with Senior Leadership

No surprises.  Bring options.  Close loops.

No surprisesKeep the Superintendent, the Treasurer, and principals informed before issues escalate.

Bring optionsDon't dump problems. Frame choices, tradeoffs, risks, and a recommendation.

Close loopsReturn with follow-up. Even if the answer is partial, show movement.

My job is to reduce noise for senior leadership, not create more of it.

04 How I Learn the Work

Ride the bus.  Walk the building.  Eat the lunch.

I learn this work from the ground up. Ride a route. Walk buildings with maintenance. Stand in a lunch line and see the flow. The spreadsheet matters, but the work teaches you what the spreadsheet misses.

05 How I Build Systems

Make the invisible visible.

Onboardingone-page checklist supervisors actually use.

Employee relationsone-page decision tree, clearly documented.

Operations · facilities · hiringone-page dashboard, shared with cabinet.

Public accountabilityone-page board brief, audit-ready.

I like simple tools that supervisors actually use. If a process only works because one person remembers all the steps, it's too fragile.

How I Think Through Any Problem

Five lenses on every question.

Every problem sorts into one of these:

PeopleWho needs clarity, support, trust, or accountability?

ProcessWhat rhythm, checklist, handoff, or documentation would reduce friction?

StewardshipWhat public dollars, public trust, safety, or sustainability is at stake?

Ground GameWhat would I learn by being physically present with the people doing the work?

SpineWhat simple structure would make the work repeatable without becoming bureaucratic?

In One Sentence

Summary

I'm a systems-minded people leader. I listen first, clarify the work, support the people closest to it, and build practical structures that help organizations serve the people they exist for.

Clear.  Grounded.  Useful.
